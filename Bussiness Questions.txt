1. Revenue Drivers

Which products and categories contribute the most to total revenue?
→ Helps focus inventory and marketing

2. Customer Value

Which customer segments generate the highest revenue? (income, education, etc.)
→ Helps target high-value customers

3. Store Performance

Which stores perform best and worst in terms of revenue and transactions?
→ Identify expansion or closure decisions

4. Time Trends

How does sales vary over time (monthly / yearly)? Are there seasonal patterns?
→ Helps forecasting and planning

5. Return Analysis

Which products have the highest return rates?
→ Signals quality or expectation issues

6. Store-level Returns

Which stores have unusually high return rates?
→ Could mean staff issues, fraud, or poor inventory

7. Customer Behavior

Do repeat customers spend more than new customers?
→ Helps retention strategy

8. Product Performance vs Returns

Are high-selling products also high-return products?
→ Classic business trade-off

9. Demographics vs Spending

How do age, income, or occupation affect purchase behavior?
→ Helps segmentation and personalization

10. Sales Efficiency

Which stores generate the highest revenue per customer?
→ Measures operational efficiency
